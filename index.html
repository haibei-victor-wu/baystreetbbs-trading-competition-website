Rules & Guidelines

  • Game Name: Bay Street Trading Competition
  • Team size: 1 - 3 people
  • Team registration fee: 35 dollar per team
  • Official Trading Period
    2016 09.12th to 10.12th NO late entry allowed
  • Initial Capital
    CAD 1,000,000 cash + USD 1,000,000 cash, deposited in one CAD account and one USD account
    Note: you can only use CAD account to buy securities that are in Canadian dollars, and use USD account to buy securities that are in U.S. dollars.
  • Financial Instruments
    Any equities, ETFs, and options that are exchange-traded and can be found on Investopedia Stock Simulator platform, including but not limited to common shares, preferred shares, ADR, ETFs, options, etc. that are listed on exchanges in North America.
  • Ending Portfolio Value
    The period-end portfolio value will be determined by the total account value (CAD account and USD account) as at the end of 2016.10.12th When calculating the total account value, the USD account value will be converted into CAD equivalents by using New York 4pm USDCAD spot exchange Rate as of 2016.10.12th.(4pm USDCAD spot exchange Rate is provided by Bloomberg. http://www.bloomberg.com/quote/USDCAD:CUR )
  • Minimum Number of Trades
    To have a valid ranking, you have to execute at least 1 trade during the competition period.
  • Delayed Quotes and Market Order Execution
    Delayed Quotes – Investopedia Simulator uses a delayed datafeed from the exchange (around 20 minutes).
    Delayed Market Order Execution – due to the delayed datafeed, we implemented a 20-minute delay in executing market orders to avoid arbitrage.
  • Margin and Short Selling
    Both margin trading and short-selling are permitted (with certain limitations). Your account must maintain a minimum margin balance to avoid getting margin call (by email) and forced liquidation. In other words, your buying power must be greater than zero. The buying power is calculated using the following formula:

    Buying Power = Total Cash + (Total Stocks * 50%) - (Shorted Stock * 150%)

    For details, refer to the links below:
    How does margin work?
    http://www.investopedia.com/simulator/help/margin.aspx

    Why did I get a margin call?
    http://www.investopedia.com/simulator/help/margincall.aspx

    I just got an email saying my account has been liquidated! Why did this happen?
    http://www.investopedia.com/simulator/help/forcedsell.aspx

    Margin interest will be charged at an annualized rate of 6.0%.

    There’re certain limitations on short-selling. Refer to section “Restrictions” for details.
  • Brokerage Commission
    Equities/ETF: $29.99 per trade
    Options: $29.99 + $5.00 per contract
  • Interest
    Cash balance: 0.0%
    Borrowed funds: 6.0%
  • Restrictions
    a) Writing options are NOT supported at the moment
    b) Mutual funds and bonds are not available for trading
    c) Quick Sell Limit - to reflect the fact that we are working with delayed data, you will not be able to sell (buy) a security within the first 15-minute period after buying (short-selling) the security. This can help prevent users from "cheating" by trading in and out of a security using real-time data.
    d) Daily Volume Limit - Trades cannot exceed 10% of volume for the day. This applies to both equities and options. This rule reduces the chance of achieving unreasonable returns by trading unrealistic amount of shares/number of contracts.
    e) Minimum Price Rule - Only securities over $1 are allowed to be traded long, and only securities over $2 are allowed to be traded short. Minimum stock for margin is only allowed for stocks over$2. Penny stocks are more prone to manipulation from stock promoters and can have erratic trading patterns.
    f) Diversification Requirement – To help prevent “gambling”, any particular options contract is limited to 10% of your starting cash. Equity position is limited to 30% of your starting cash.
    g) In order to avoid copying the same trading strategies from other participants, the portfolio holding information is not viewable to other people.

  • Competition conference rules
    The top 10 performer will be able to move forward to the final investment conference, where each participant will be provided with 5 minutes presentation to present his or her investment strategy/philosophy used in the past month. The winner will be decided by both the previous month’s performance and the stock pitches performance judged by the industry professional. The conference will be concluded by presenting the first/second/ third place winner each for one candidate.
    A) Top 10 performance is evaluated by the ending portfolio balance after the currency conversion as described in the above ending portfolio value.
    B) The final conference’s grade will be determined by the below formula:
    10% for the previous one month stock return ranking, the maximum value is 10 and the minimum is 1, then assigned weight 10%.
    90% for the stock pitches score provided by the judger (simple average of all scores supplied), then assigned the weight 90%.
